# Probabilistic Decision Making

Framing all decisions as probability distributions, not binary outcomes. Core to [[Jiwoong Kim]]'s epistemic stance.

## Principles
- "Perfect validation is impossible" — accept and quantify uncertainty
- Model performance = historically conditioned probabilistic evidence
- Communicate in probability ranges, sensitivity plots, not binary yes/no
- Adaptive: update beliefs from new evidence (Bayesian-flavored)

## Applications
- [[ML-Modeling]]: treat model outputs as probability distributions
- [[Signal-Validation]]: probability of signal persistence, not certainty
- [[Evidence-First-Communication]]: convey uncertainty honestly to PMs
- [[Model-Robustness-Testing]]: probability across regimes
