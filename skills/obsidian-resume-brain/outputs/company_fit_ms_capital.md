## 3. Intern Role Appropriateness

**Concern: PhD + 1 year industry experience → Intern title?**

| Factor | Assessment |
|--------|-----------|
| **Title mismatch** | ⚠️ Significant. PhD + live fund management + benchmark-beating track record typically commands full Researcher or at minimum Junior Researcher title |
| **Singapore norms** | Some SG quant firms use "intern" loosely as a trial/probation period (3-6 months) before converting to full-time |
| **Small firm context** | Early-stage firms sometimes use intern titles to manage costs or de-risk hiring |
| **Candidate's track record** | Operated a live mutual fund with +43.54% returns. This is not intern-level experience. |

**Verdict:** The intern title is a **yellow flag**. The candidate should clarify:
1. Is this a trial-to-hire arrangement? What's the conversion path?
2. What is the compensation structure?
3. What is the expected duration?

If it's a 3-month trial with clear conversion to full Researcher, acceptable. If it's a prolonged intern role, it undervalues the candidate significantly.

- **Role-level fit: 30/100** — Intern title is inappropriate for PhD + demonstrated alpha generation. Significant underleveling.

**Summary:** The company's DNA (AI/ML quant, small team, research-focused) aligns well with the candidate's compass vectors. However, the intern title is a poor match for someone who has a PhD and has already operated a live fund beating benchmarks by ~8%. The candidate should either **(a)** negotiate for a Researcher title, or **(b)** pursue this only if it's a clearly defined short trial with guaranteed conversion and competitive compensation. Without those assurances, the opportunity cost is too high — the candidate should target full Quant Researcher roles at comparable or larger firms.
